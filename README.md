# 📊 Employee Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations as it affects productivity, hiring costs, and team stability.

In this project, I performed an **Employee Attrition Analysis** to identify the factors that influence why employees leave an organization.

The analysis focuses on **demographic factors, job-related factors, work experience, and income-related variables** to uncover patterns that contribute to employee turnover.

---

## 🎯 Objective
The main objective of this project is to:

- Understand **employee attrition patterns**
- Identify **key factors affecting employee turnover**
- Provide **data-driven insights for improving employee retention**

---

## 🗂 Dataset Information
The dataset contains information about employees including:

- Age
- Department
- Job Role
- Monthly Income
- Work Life Balance
- Job Satisfaction
- Overtime
- Distance From Home
- Total Working Years
- Years at Company
- Attrition Status (Yes / No)

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning
Before performing the analysis, the dataset was cleaned using the following steps:

- Checked and handled **missing values**
- Removed **duplicate records**
- Verified **data types and formats**
- Created grouped categories where required

Clean data ensured the **accuracy and reliability of the analysis**.

---

## 📊 Data Analysis Method
The analysis was performed using **Excel Pivot Tables** to summarize the data and identify patterns.

Different pivot tables were created to analyze:

- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Age
- Attrition by Overtime
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Work Life Balance
- Attrition by Job Satisfaction
- Attrition by Distance From Home
- Monthly Income Distribution by Attrition

All charts were combined into a **single analysis sheet dashboard** for better visualization.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 1️⃣ Overtime Impact
Employees who work overtime show **higher attrition rates**, suggesting workload may influence employee turnover.

### 2️⃣ Age Factor
Employees in the **25–34 age group** show higher attrition compared to other age groups.

### 3️⃣ Department Trends
Attrition varies across departments, with **sales-related roles showing higher turnover**.

### 4️⃣ Salary Influence
Employees with **lower monthly income are more likely to leave the organization**.

### 5️⃣ Experience Level
Employees with **shorter tenure in the company tend to leave more frequently**.

### 6️⃣ Distance From Home
Employees living farther from the workplace show slightly **higher attrition tendencies**.

## 📌 Conclusion
The analysis shows that **employee attrition is influenced by multiple factors including salary, workload, work-life balance, experience level, and demographic characteristics**.

Organizations can reduce attrition by improving:

- Compensation structures
- Work-life balance
- Career growth opportunities
- Employee satisfaction
